About Me

I am currently a Prime Minister's research fellow (PMRF) at the department of Mathematics, Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, India. My research broadly lies in the heart of Probability and Statistics. My research advisor is Prof. Srikanth K. Iyer. The random patterns of objects really fascinates me and is the driving force of my research. Recently, I have grown interests in machine learning and coding. Besides pen and paper, I enjoy playing table tennis, frisbee, mafia (werewolf), and video games specially Fifa.

Research

My research interests include stochastic geometry, random network structures, random geometric graphs, continuum percolation, first-passage percolation, last-passage percolation.
